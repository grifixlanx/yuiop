Roman Society & Culture

Life in ancient Rome revolved around the city of Rome, its famed seven hills, and its monumental architecture such as the Colosseum, the Forum of Trajan, and the Pantheon. Imperial Roman society was highly stratified, deeply shifting the dynamics of daily life between different social legal classes.

The structure of Roman society determined everything from political power to dress codes and legal protections:

  • Patricians vs. Plebeians: Patricians were the wealthy, land-owning noble elite who held hereditary political power. Plebeians constituted the massive general body of free Roman citizens, working as farmers, artisans, and traders.
  • Class Structure: Below the free citizens were freedmen (former slaves who had been legally emancipated) and a vast population of enslaved individuals who provided the core labor force for agriculture, mining, and domestic estates.
  • The Family Unit: The head of the household was the paterfamilias (the oldest living male), who held absolute legal authority over his wife, children, and household slaves.

Roman culture left an enduring legacy on the Western world. Their sophisticated legal codes formed the foundation for civil law systems globally, while Latin evolved into the modern Romance languages and shaped scientific, legal, and theological vocabulary for millennia.
